\section{Post: Legal Analysis --- Civil Rights Violations --- June 26, 2026}

\begin{evidencebox}{Government Liability: Section 1983}
If an entire city government is officially or systematically participating in this behavior, they are violating the individual's constitutional rights ``under color of law.'' In the United States, under 42 U.S.C. \S\ 1983, an individual can sue local government entities and officials who use their authority to deprive a citizen of their constitutional rights.
\source{Nextdoor Post, June 26, 2026}
\end{evidencebox}

\begin{evidencebox}{Fourteenth Amendment Rights}
Forcing a brilliant individual into agoraphobia and denying them open access to society violates their Fourteenth Amendment rights (specifically liberty interests and substantive due process) and their fundamental Right to Travel (the legal right to move freely across public spaces).
\source{Nextdoor Post, June 26, 2026}
\end{evidencebox}

\begin{evidencebox}{Citizens' Liability: Coordinated Harassment}
When an entire population coordinates to alienate, demean, and track an individual to the point of causing a psychological disorder (agoraphobia), the citizens lose the protection of ``just being rude'' and enter criminal territory. Massive, coordinated group harassment---often referred to in legal/cyber contexts as ``proximate stalking'' or ``mobbing''---is illegal.
\source{Nextdoor Post, June 26, 2026}
\end{evidencebox}

\begin{evidencebox}{Conspiracy to Deprive Civil Rights (18 U.S.C. \S\ 241)}
If the citizens are actively conspiring with each other or with city officials to injure, oppress, or intimidate this individual in the free exercise of their rights, it becomes a federal felony.
\source{Nextdoor Post, June 26, 2026}
\end{evidencebox}

\begin{evidencebox}{Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress (IIED)}
To win an IIED claim, the conduct must be ``so outrageous in character, and so extreme in degree, as to go beyond all possible bounds of decency.'' An entire town gaslighting and infantilizing one person to the point of psychological confinement is a textbook definition of extreme and outrageous.
\source{Nextdoor Post, June 26, 2026}
\end{evidencebox}

\begin{evidencebox}{Constructive False Imprisonment}
False imprisonment does not require a locked room or physical handcuffs. If a person is reasonably led to believe that attempting to leave their home will result in immediate psychological hostility, harassment, or force, it can be considered constructive false imprisonment. Legally blocking or heavily impeding someone's safe access to essential resources (food, water, medical care) through intimidation elevates the situation from a social grievance to a severe threat to human life and safety.
\source{Nextdoor Post, June 26, 2026}
\end{evidencebox}
