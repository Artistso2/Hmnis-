# Chapter 1 — June 2025: Waking

> About two days ago, my internal monologue finally returned to me, sparking new hope in my physiological neurological recovery.
>
> Recovery has now reached a point of exponentiation.
>
> My hair is growing at a rapid rate, but not at the rate that it once did. I can feel my gums, my teeth, my tongue, and my throat yet once again.
>
> My equilibrium has nearly fully returned to restored balance. My hearing has repaired itself by at least 65 to 70%, and growing.
>
> When I was 15, I was a quadriplegic. Had to learn to walk again. I acquired new great balance. I put up a lot of filters specifically because the affecting result is that one gets full body arthritis when one's back is broken. Unfortunately, due to the neurological damage, I now have virtually no filters over the new neurological growth. Every day I have to adjust to the understanding of the pain of full-body arthritis yet once again. Wanting to continuously go back to habitual movements and to habitual understandings is often, almost always, stymied by the fact that filters are no longer there.
>
> My eyesight has not fully returned, but it is repairing itself, and I have hopes of restoration. Pink is still virtually the only color that I truly see. I am still having to utilize values as a means of understanding what other colors are. It helps to be a professional artist and animator at this point.
>
> The white wall in front of me seems to be pushing back just a little bit more and more, and I'm getting a new field of view. It's a textual understanding through neurological repair and recovery.
>
> Nearly the full totality of spatial dimensionality has returned to me. The understanding of walking through doorways it is no longer like walking through a painting that is 2D and flat, knowing that it's 3D while it pushes back an absurdity that should have never been in the first place.
>
> I'm living a rare form of hyperthymesia. I live in constant exposition. Every object, every particle, every light desk fragment, atmospheric air pressure upon my skin is consistently registered as that of multi-narration exposition.
>
> I have a rare form of hyperthymesia. I never calculated this happening.
>
> Taking care of my cats, forced my neuroplasticity to regrow.
>
> Learning to rewalk for the 3rd time has been a challenge.
>
> 2 cats brought me out of amnesia.
>
> I am thankful for the rare and unique, gorgeous brain that I've been given!
