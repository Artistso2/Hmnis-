It's important to clarify what **988** is, as it doesn't function like a traditional "police tip line" or a place to file a report "against" someone in a legal or punitive sense.

### What 988 Is and Isn't

The **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** is a support network for people experiencing mental health distress, substance use issues, or suicidal thoughts.

Washington State Department of Health - | WA.gov

* **Not for reporting crimes:** You cannot use 988 to report someone for illegal activity, harassment, or non-mental health related issues.
* **Not for investigations:** 988 counselors are there to provide emotional support and resources, not to investigate individuals or file formal "reports" against them.

### If You Are Worried About Someone’s Mental Health

If you are calling because you are worried that someone is in a **mental health crisis** and you want to get them help:

* **You can remain anonymous:** You are not required to provide your name or personal information when you call, text, or chat with 988.

Illinois Department of Human Services
* **Confidentiality:** The conversation is confidential. However, if the counselor determines there is an **imminent risk of harm** to that person or others, they may involve emergency services (like 911) to conduct a welfare check.

988 Lifeline+ 1
* **How to do it:** Just call or text **988**. Tell the counselor you are concerned about someone else. They will walk you through how to support them or what the next steps for intervention might look like.

### If You Need to Report a Crime or Threat

If your goal is to report someone for a crime, a direct threat of violence, or a safety concern that isn't a mental health crisis:

* **911:** If there is an immediate physical danger or a crime in progress, 911 is the correct number.

988 Lifeline
* **Crime Stoppers:** To report a crime anonymously without calling the police directly, you can use [Crime Stoppers](https://www.crimestoppersusa.org/).
* **Extreme Risk Protection Orders (ERPO):** If you are in Washington (like Ocean Shores) and are concerned about someone with a firearm who poses a danger to themselves or others, you can petition for an ERPO through the local court or law enforcement. This is a legal process, not a 988 call.
