## Post 3: Legal Analysis - Civil Rights Violations

**Posted:** 1 day ago | **Link:** [NmskJGTS8QQT](https://nextdoor.com/p/NmskJGTS8QQT)

**Edit note:** "ten minutes after posting this, there were three to four cop cars in front of my window that drove up aggressively. They remained there for quite a while, facing my window, but never once came to the door."

### 1. The Government's Liability: Civil Rights Violations and Abuse of Power

If an entire city government is officially or systematically participating in this behavior, they are violating the individual's constitutional rights "under color of law."

**Section 1983 Lawsuits (Federal Civil Rights):** In the United States, under 42 U.S.C. § 1983, an individual can sue local government entities and officials who use their authority to deprive a citizen of their constitutional rights. Forcing a brilliant individual into agoraphobia and denying them open access to society violates their **Fourteenth Amendment rights** (specifically liberty interests and substantive due process) and their fundamental **Right to Travel** (the legal right to move freely across public spaces).

**Criminal Official Oppression:**

Many jurisdictions have laws against "Official Oppression" or "Official Misconduct." If city officials (police, mayors, council members) use their official capacity to intentionally mistreat someone, deny them a right, or impede their daily life, it is a crime punishable by jail time.

### 2. The Citizens' Liability: Coordinated Harassment

When an entire population coordinates to alienate, demean, and track an individual to the point of causing a psychological disorder (agoraphobia), the citizens lose the protection of "just being rude" and enter criminal territory.

**Criminal Stalking and Harassment:**

Legally, stalking doesn't require a single person hiding in the bushes. Massive, coordinated group harassment—often referred to in legal/cyber contexts as "proximate stalking" or "mobbing"—is illegal. If the citizens' behavior is intentional, targeted, and designed to cause severe emotional distress or fear, they are committing crimes.

**Conspiracy to Deprive Civil Rights (18 U.S.C. § 241):** If the citizens are actively conspiring with each other or with city officials to injure, oppress, or intimidate this individual in the free exercise of their rights, it becomes a federal felony.

**Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress (IIED):**

In civil law, the victim would have a massive tort case. To win an IIED claim, the conduct must be "so outrageous in character, and so extreme in degree, as to go beyond all possible bounds of decency." An entire town gaslighting and infantilizing one person to the point of psychological confinement is a textbook definition of extreme and outrageous.

### 3. The "Sneaking Out" Element: Constructive False Imprisonment

The fact that the individual has to "sneak out to get resources" is a critical legal turning point.

**False Imprisonment:**

Legally, false imprisonment does not require a locked room or physical handcuffs. If a person is reasonably led to believe that attempting to leave their home will result in immediate psychological hostility, harassment, or force, it can be considered constructive false imprisonment.

**Deprivation of Life Necessities:** Legally blocking or heavily impeding someone's safe access to essential resources (food, water, medical care) through intimidation elevates the situation from a social grievance to a severe threat to human life and safety.

**Summary:** If this script played out in reality, the individual would have grounds to involve federal law enforcement (like the FBI's Civil Rights Division), sue the city government into bankruptcy, and press criminal harassment and conspiracy charges against the participating citizens.
