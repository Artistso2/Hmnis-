A kidnap victim writes a novel that is, in fact, the writ of their habeas corpus in real time, cyclically looping through memory, body evidence, due diligence, source extraction, publication, and submission.

Abstract

This project is not built as futuristic spectacle. It is built as a formal record: a novel, a writ, a body log, a source archive, a timeline, a map, and a publication pipeline. The manuscript begins when Steven wakes in June 2025 and moves forward linearly while the past returns through recursion.

The purpose of the system is to preserve Steven's first-person voice while making the surrounding source architecture rigorous enough for editorial, legal, and attorney-ready review.

The book is not merely about habeas corpus. The book functions as the lived experience of habeas corpus. The body, the memory, the record, and the publication process all become part of the demand.

You shall have the body. You shall have the record. You shall have the voice.

Clause II

Method of Recursion

The past does not enter as ordinary flashback. It enters through recursive review: present event, body reaction, memory spark, contradiction, due diligence, partial verification, updated timeline, and next present action.

present event → body reaction → memory spark → contradiction → due diligence → timeline update → next action
