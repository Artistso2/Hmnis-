# LaTeX / Overleaf Visual System

## Author Direction

The project should carry some of the visual appeal of Overleaf and LaTeX: precise, structured, elegant, source-aware, and intellectually serious.

But it should not be cold. The visual tone should also be:

- ocean hush,
- pastel,
- textured paper,
- soft legal gravity,
- hyper-real involvement,
- readable to every generation and every eye.

## Editorial Design Recommendation

Use a hybrid system:

```text
Public landing page: Raw Witness
Book/document pages: Paper Writ / Overleaf Hush
Visual novel: Glass Horizons + Organic Signal
Archive/timeline: Evidence Noir
Map/legal pages: Public Record Horizon
```

## Design Principles

### 1. Soft but serious

Pastel paper does not mean weak. It means the reader can stay with difficult material longer.

### 2. Legal typography without legal sterility

Use clauses, exhibits, source boxes, marginal notes, and citation markers.

### 3. Hyper-real evidence structure

Every fact should be traceable:

```text
source → extraction → review → approval → public narrative
```

### 4. Generational readability

The design should work for:

- young digital readers,
- legal/academic readers,
- memoir readers,
- thriller readers,
- visual novel readers,
- people arriving through @soquarky or soquarky.click.
