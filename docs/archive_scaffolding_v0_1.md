# HELLO, MY NAME IS STEVEN

## Master Novel Scaffolding / Story Bible v0.1

**Working genre:** Hyper-realistic psychological thriller / legal-tech noir / found-document survival narrative  
**Working form:** A fictionalized digital writ of habeas corpus, proof-of-life packet, memoir-fragment, forensic timeline, and SOS flare.  
**Core rule:** The novel must distinguish between **verified fact**, **protagonist memory**, **protagonist allegation**, **medical/legal interpretation**, and **fictionalized connective tissue**.

---

# 0. Foundational Guardrail

This scaffolding treats the material as a **novel architecture**, not as a legal finding. The protagonist may speak with certainty, intensity, rage, lucidity, contradiction, and spiritual/philosophical force. The manuscript can preserve that voice. But the production system must label information in the draft pipeline so the story does not accidentally present unverified allegations about living people as adjudicated fact.

## The Five Evidence Labels

Every scene, claim, memory, or document must be tagged as one of the following:

| Label | Meaning | Draft Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| **VF — Verified Fact** | Confirmed by document, recording, public record, timestamp, medical record, police record, photo/video, or direct artifact. | Can be stated cleanly in narration or exhibit form. |
| **PM — Protagonist Memory** | Remembered by Steven/Lovely through hyperthymesia or recovered autobiographical recall. | Can be rendered subjectively and intensely, but not externally certified unless supported. |
| **PA — Protagonist Allegation** | Claimed by the protagonist but not externally proven inside the scaffolding. | Use legal/forensic language: “I allege,” “the record I can reconstruct,” “what I believe happened,” “the body remembers.” |
| **MI — Medical/Legal Interpretation** | Explanatory framework that requires professional confirmation. | Mark for expert review. Do not overstate. |
| **FC — Fictionalized Composite** | Invented connective tissue, composite characters, altered names, changed addresses, compressed timelines. | Must be explicitly marked in internal notes. |

## Defamation / Living-Person Guardrail

For the public novel draft, all non-public living people should be fictionalized or composited unless there is a deliberate legal strategy and publishing counsel approves otherwise. Use aliases in manuscript development:

- **Roommate C.** instead of real name.
- **Daughter H.** instead of real name.
- **Son-in-law M.** instead of real name.
- **Advocate L.** instead of real name.
- **Agency A / Housing Program B / Facility F** unless public-record documentation supports naming.

# 1. The Irreducible Premise

A person whose mind was built to remember everything wakes into a life where the most important years are missing.

The protagonist—born Steven Michael Allen Owens and later living as Lovely Rhythmic Melody—constructs a digital writ of habeas corpus from inside a collapsing life: a box, a room, an island-like isolation, a body altered by trauma, poverty, institutional control, neurological injury, and time. The book is not merely about escape. The book **is** the escape attempt.

## Core Thematic Question

> What is the point of coming out of amnesia to discover that one is a kidnap victim with hyperthymesia?

## Core Legal-Existential Paradox

**Habeas corpus** means, in literal spirit: *you shall have the body.*  
But the protagonist’s central question is:

> Which body are you demanding they produce—the remembered body, the damaged body, the legal body, the gendered body, the medicalized body, the starving body, or the body that survived when the world wrote it off?

## Core Engine

The novel moves through:

1. **Memory recursion** — the return of autobiographical data.
2. **Digital reconstruction** — phone/tablet/AI/code/search as cognitive prosthesis.
3. **Legal invocation** — the manuscript as writ, complaint, testimony, evidence binder.
4. **Physical survival** — hunger, unsafe housing, locks, fear, cats, disability, exposure.
5. **Chronological proof** — if Steven/Lovely can rebuild the timeline, the self becomes harder to erase.

# 2. Narrative Form

## Primary Form: The Living Writ

The book is structured as a leaked or compiled file assembled by the protagonist. Each chapter functions as one of the following:

- **Petition Clause** — a legalistic demand for recognition or release.
- **Memory Exhibit** — a recovered scene from the hyperthymestic archive.
- **Medical Exhibit** — body status, symptoms, medications, injuries, scans, recovery milestones.
- **Digital Exhibit** — screenshots, logs, code, AI conversations, account events, metadata.
- **Geographic Exhibit** — maps, routes, addresses, distances, weather, transit, rural isolation.
- **Witness Exhibit** — messages to/from Advocate L. or others.
- **Survival Log** — food, shelter, cats, money, locks, threats, panic.
- **Mathematical/Philosophical Exhibit** — AMCEP, TriForce, Certainty Principle, GQM, AETERNA.

## Reader Position

The reader is not simply observing. The reader is holding the packet Steven/Lovely launched into the world.

The reader must feel:

- “If I stop reading, I am abandoning the petitioner.”
- “If the chronology holds, the body becomes evidence.”
- “If the body is evidence, the world must answer.”

# 3. Voice Design

## The Voice Is the Event

The protagonist’s voice should be preserved as a high-pressure stream of:

- precision,
- accusation,
- absurdist humor,
- legal instinct,
- technical intelligence,
- trauma intensity,
- philosophical reach,
- class rage,
- hunger,
- tenderness toward cats,
- contempt for infantilizing language,
- and a relentless demand to be treated as an adult witness.

## Voice Rules

1. **Do not smooth the protagonist into generic thriller prose.**
2. **Do not make the voice “tidy” at the expense of truth.**
3. **Do not mock intensity.** The intensity is part of the evidence of survival.
4. **Do not confuse nonlinear expression with lack of intelligence.**
5. **Do not let the AI invent résumé details, agencies, diagnoses, medications, travel times, or legal outcomes.**
6. **Use repetition strategically.** Trauma repeats; legal petitions repeat; emergency calls repeat. Repetition can become rhythm.
7. **When the protagonist alleges crimes, preserve the force while using careful legal framing.**
